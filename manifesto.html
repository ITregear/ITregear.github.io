Engineering Manifesto

  1. Engineer like a scientist

    Always question your own hypotheses. Prototypes should be made to prove that your ideas don’t work. Only when you can’t break them, or find flaws, have you hit the jackpot.

  2. Never fall victim to sunk cost fallacy

    Learn to realise when to change course, even if it means completely changing direction. Incomplete work isn’t a loss, it's a lesson. Learn when not to persevere.

  3. Don’t waste time on ‘cool features’

    Save these for your hobbyist projects. For a project to succeed you need to be honest with yourself and strictly work on only what genuinely adds value.

  4. An inventor should always be willing to use their own product

    Drive the car you work on. Clean the machine you’ve designed. You’ll never know what aspects truly need your attention unless you’ve had to suffer at the hands of your own oversights. Get off your desk.

  5. Prototype as fast as you can

    The rate of success is directly proportional to your rate of iteration. Don’t build a full product prototype just to test one feature. Make your experiments as small and fast as possible. If your workflow requires weeks to receive feedback, change it.

  6. No future-proofing

    There’s no point in imagining all the ways your product might not work, wasting time trying to mitigate them, when you might never even get there. Wait for success, and then play catchup.
